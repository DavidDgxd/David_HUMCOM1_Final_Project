‚úçÔ∏è Reflection: Human-Computer Interaction

HCI has changed my interest in technology - from coding software that works to designing experiences for the user. Initially, I thought of web development as learning syntax - but the lessons in usability, affordance, and accessibility added a human dimension to my work.

The term "affordance refers to the cues provided by an object (either physical or digital) that tell us how to use it. The takeaway for me was that every design choice we make conveys something about what that object does. By learning that even small details like button shape affect perceived functionality, it became much easier for me to evaluate and create user interfaces. I could apply the lessons from this experience directly to my portfolio, specifically regarding my portfolio project, the "game inventory," as it had to communicate quite clearly how to navigate and interact with the game inventory through affordances.

I found that exploring accessibility was a huge part of this course. Before taking this course, I didn't think much about those with visual, auditory, or cognitive impairments. Group activities, such as the creation of Accessibility Solutions Mind Map, showed me how ethically developers are responsible for creating inclusive designs. Good design includes making systems usable by everyone, not only creating aesthetically pleasing pieces.

Applying heuristic evaluation methods and contrasting traditional teamwork with digital collaboration further improved my understanding. Learning to evaluate interfaces using Nielsen's principles provided a vocabulary for articulating what makes a design effective or frustrating.

In conclusion, HCI has provided me with the theoretical and practical background needed to fill the gap between technical execution and human needs. I no longer just see code; I see users, and that mindset is crucial for my career in Computer Science. This course has instilled a commitment to creating systems that are not only powerful but also intuitive, efficient, and equitable.
